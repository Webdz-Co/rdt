Helps children learn the fun of making a plan for their money!

Rebecca Turner has a delightful way of telling a story that is so appealing to children! This story is an adventure for children to learn how to manage their money--learned when then are young and then continuing into their later years. How I wish I had had this book when I was young--and even when my children were young! It makes money management such fun and so understandable. It also guides children into decisions that helps them to know the joy of sharing what we have with others, and how to save for things that are important to them. A wonderfully helpful book. -- REVIEW OF DIMES by JAN MILLER

Great adoption story

This is a wonderful book! Who is this book for? Everyone--adopted children, parents who have adopted, those who have given their children for adoption, the adult who was adopted, anyone who knows anyone who has been adopted! There is a story for children which makes them understand that they are not different from other children, but that they are also unique and Chosen. It also promotes questioning. The pictures are wonderful. There is also a real story for adults where the author has invited us to share in her own experience of growing up in a loving family as an adopted child who found her birth mother. She also offers resources the reader might find helpful. I will gladly share this book with colleagues and friends I know who have adopted children and with others. -- REVIEW OF CHOSEN by JN

Wish this had been available for my son many years ago

What a wonderful book! As all books and things they are open for interpretation. My interpretation of the 2 dad's and mom's is this. Many years ago after a divorce (marriage blessed me with a wonderful son) when I remarried my new husband wanted to adopt my son. The biological dad had no problem letting my new husband adopt his son. 45 years later as my husband and I and my ex husband and his wife attend birthdays, graduations,weddings, etc. that involve my son and grandchildren, my son enjoys these events with his 2 dad's and 2 moms. How blessed he is to have such a big family! I so appreciate and enjoyed this book that I have passed several copies on to friends that have adopted children. I am taking one to my Doctor this next week that has recently adopted a beautiful baby girl! Congratulations to all these families and children so blessed. REVIEW OF CHOSEN by MARSHA M

The truth rings true

I read Becky's book from the perspective of a trained and licensed social worker. WOW, what a difference this book has made in reigniting my passion for developing the Human Factor in the non-profit arena, particularly in homeless shelters. Becky alludes to the incredible lack of the Human Factor regarding the issue of homelessness on page 34 by stating it is hard for most of us to walk in another person's shoes, especially with people whose lives are so inherently different. I have worked on the front lines of homelessness and this population needs the touch of the Human Factor. That means you and me. Becky sums up the chapter on The Human Factor by leaving each of us to contemplate this one area where we can all give back to humanity. Ultimately, there are many areas we can all give back to. We just have to open our eyes and become aware of the issues around us. Thanks for the reminder, Becky. What a breath of fresh air that a businesswoman writes a book that speaks to all of us from all walks of life. -- REVIEW OF VIRUS by SUSAN A. LOVE
